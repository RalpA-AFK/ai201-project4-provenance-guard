------------------------------------------------------------
BEAT 1 — THE PROBLEM  (~15 sec, just talk)
------------------------------------------------------------
[SAY]
"Provenance Guard checks whether text was written by a human or AI.
Perfect AI detection is unsolved, and on a writing platform falsely
accusing a human is the worst outcome — so instead of forcing a
yes/no, this system scores its confidence and is honest when it
doesn't know."


------------------------------------------------------------
BEAT 2 — CLEARLY HUMAN TEXT  (~30 sec)
------------------------------------------------------------
[SAY] "Here's a casual first-person post."

   >>>>>>  COPY-PASTE (Terminal 2 / git bash)  >>>>>>
curl -s -X POST http://localhost:5000/submit -H "Content-Type: application/json" -d '{"text":"ok so i finally tried that new ramen place downtown and honestly? underwhelming. the broth was fine but WAY too much sodium and i was thirsty for like three hours after. probably wont go back unless someone drags me","creator_id":"demo-human"}' | python -m json.tool
   <<<<<<  end  <<<<<<

[POINT] attribution = "likely_human", high confidence, and the
        plain-language label a reader would see.
[SAY]   "Both signals agree it reads human, so it sits confidently
        in the human band."


------------------------------------------------------------
BEAT 3 — FORMAL HUMAN TEXT  (the key demo)  (~30 sec)
------------------------------------------------------------
[SAY] "Now formal human writing — the hard case."

   >>>>>>  COPY-PASTE (Terminal 2 / git bash)  >>>>>>
curl -s -X POST http://localhost:5000/submit -H "Content-Type: application/json" -d '{"text":"The relationship between monetary policy and asset price inflation has been extensively studied in the literature. Central banks face a fundamental tension between price stability and prolonged low interest rates.","creator_id":"demo-formal"}' | python -m json.tool
   <<<<<<  end  <<<<<<

[POINT] attribution = "uncertain".
[SAY]   "Stylometry sees long, uniform, sophisticated sentences and
        thinks AI. The language model is less sure. Because the two
        signals disagree, my scoring pulls the result toward the
        middle — 'uncertain' — instead of falsely calling a real
        human's work AI. That disagreement rule is the core design
        decision."

------------------------------------------------------------
BEAT 6 — ONE DESIGN DECISION TO CLOSE  (~20 sec, just talk)
------------------------------------------------------------
[SAY]
"Two things I'm happy with. First, two genuinely independent signals
— one reads meaning, one measures structure — so a confident verdict
needs both to agree. Second, the thresholds are asymmetric: I require
0.80 to call something AI but only 0.30 to call it human, because on
a writing platform a false accusation is far worse than a miss. The
whole system is designed to say 'I don't know' rather than be
confidently wrong."


============================================================
QUICK REFERENCE — SCORES TO EXPECT
============================================================
Casual human   -> likely_human   (ai_score ~0.19, confidence ~0.61)
Formal human   -> uncertain      (ai_score ~0.72, confidence ~0.45)
Strong AI text -> likely_ai      (ai_score ~0.80+)
Rate limit     -> 200 x10, then 429

(The LLM signal can vary slightly; exact numbers may shift a little.)
